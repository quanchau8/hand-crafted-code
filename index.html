Our Conviction

On writing every character

We hold a simple conviction. A line of code deserves the full attention of the person writing it.

Somewhere along the way, the industry began to accept suggestions. Grey text appeared at the end of the cursor, and programmers learned to press Tab. We understand the appeal. We decline it anyway.

In our atelier, every character is chosen. Our artisans write in silence, from memory and from feel, in the manner of their teachers and of their teachers before them. When one of them opens a file, nothing is predicted on their behalf and nothing is completed on their behalf. If a function takes all afternoon, it takes all afternoon. The afternoon was going to pass regardless.

We make one promise. Every line was meant.

The Method

Our process

  1. I

    Sourcing

    We recruit developers with no history of assisted completion. Each candidate writes a linked list on paper, in pencil, under observation. Fewer than one in two hundred is invited to the bench.

  2. II

    Resting

    Freshly written code rests overnight in a cool, dark branch. This allows the logic to settle and the off-by-ones to rise gently to the surface, where they belong.

  3. III

    Signing

    Every git blame is signed by hand. The author's name appears in the history because a person put it there, on purpose, and was present when it happened.

  4. IV

    Review by candlelight

    Batches of no more than forty lines are read aloud by candlelight until the room agrees. Some batches are never agreed upon. Those are composted.

Provenance

Heritage features

Machine-produced code is uniform by design. Ours carries the marks of the hands and seasons behind it. Connoisseurs will recognize the following signatures of authenticity.

Trace technical debt

Contains trace amounts of technical debt, as nature intended. We filter nothing. We clarify nothing. What settles at the bottom of the repository is part of the experience.

The load-bearing global

Somewhere in every batch lives a single global variable named after the artisan's cat. It holds the entire structure up. We ask only that you treat it with respect.

The untouched function

One function arrives marked // do not touch. It has passed intact through four generations of maintainers. It works. The reasons are no longer recorded anywhere.

Terroir naming

Variable names express the conditions of their vintage: temp, tempFINAL, actualTemp_USE_THIS_ONE. No two harvests read alike.
